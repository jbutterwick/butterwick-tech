I’m Jordan Butterwick, a Senior Software Engineer based in Airdrie, Alberta. I currently work at Tresco Consoles, where I focus on cloud efficiency, engineering workflow, system design, and mentoring. My work tends to sit where product needs meet difficult technical constraints: systems that have outgrown their original shape, data that needs to become dependable, and teams that need a clear path from an idea to production.

## Building systems that can grow

My career has moved through payments, machine learning, data platforms, event-driven systems, and cloud infrastructure. At Helcim, I helped form the Data and ML team and worked on fraud defence and payment APIs. At Statflo, I was a founding engineer on the Data and Analytics team, working with billions of records and services processing millions of daily events. At DocuPet, I helped modernize a legacy platform as it grew from 500,000 to more than 15 million users.

Those environments taught me to treat architecture as an operating discipline rather than a diagram. I care about clear boundaries, observable systems, manageable delivery paths, and designs that the next engineer can understand. I’m equally comfortable starting a focused greenfield service, carefully unpicking a monolith, or helping a small team establish the habits needed to own production software.

## How I work

I like direct communication, written decisions, small reversible steps, and evidence from running systems. Technical leadership, to me, includes reducing ambiguity, making trade-offs visible, and helping other engineers develop sound judgement—not simply choosing technologies. Recent work includes cutting AWS spend by 75%, designing software-development and agentic-engineering workflows, and mentoring junior engineers on system design and engineering practice.

## Outside work

Away from a keyboard, I maintain a large garden, fish, hike, whittle, and write about projects that help me learn. Those interests keep this site personal as well as professional.
